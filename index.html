Variant 1 keeps the earlier solution untouched: "Require approval for all quotes" and "Require approval based on Tier Pricing deviation" are mutually exclusive and hide/disable each other; the normal criterion configurator disappears while either is on; "Bypass approval for quotes revisions" is fully independent with its own blue box.

Variant 2 adds the native-feeling affordances from the requirements: a simulated tenant-level "Tier Pricing" setting that locks the rule's Tiered Price toggle with an inline hint when off; an "or" indicator between the two mutually-exclusive toggles (not shown near Bypass, since it isn't part of that exclusion); the standard criterion configurator now lives directly under "Require approval for all quotes" as removable cards ("+ Add threshold criteria" appends another), and only appears while that toggle is off and Tiered Price is not selected — it's fully hidden, not just dimmed, once either toggle is on; each tier direction (below/above) now has its own $/% unit toggle and a required Deviation threshold; and a warning when the tenant-level toggle is turned off while rules still reference Tiered Price, linking to the affected rule(s). Steps 1–3, the rules table, and the deactivate-rule flow remain out of scope in both variants.
